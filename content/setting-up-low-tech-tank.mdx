Setting up a planted tank doesn't have to be expensive or complicated. In this guide, we'll walk through the process of creating a stunning "low tech" aquascape.

## What is a Low Tech Tank?

A low tech tank is an aquarium that relies on natural processes rather than pressurized CO2 and high-intensity lighting. It's slower-growing, easier to maintain, and perfect for beginners!

### The Essentials

1. **Substrate**: Use a nutrient-rich soil capped with gravel or sand.
2. **Lighting**: A standard LED light (around 15-20 lumens per liter).
3. **Plants**: Choose hardy plants like Anubias, Java Fern, and Cryptocorynes.

### Step-by-Step Setup

- **Step 1:** Lay down your substrate. Slope it higher at the back for depth.
- **Step 2:** Place your hardscape (rocks and driftwood).
- **Step 3:** Plant your aquatic plants carefully using tweezers.
- **Step 4:** Fill the tank slowly to avoid disturbing the soil.
- **Step 5:** Turn on the filter and let it cycle for a few weeks!

> Always be patient. Nature takes time to find its balance.
