African Cichlids are some of the most colorful, active, and behaviorally fascinating fish you can keep in a home aquarium. Hailing from the Rift Lakes of East Africa—primarily Lake Malawi and Lake Tanganyika—these fish demand specific setups to thrive.

## Designing the Perfect Hardscape

Cichlids love rocks. In their native habitats, they live among rocky reefs, using cracks and crevices for shelter, breeding, and establishing territory. 

- **Structure**: Stack rocks securely. Cichlids are notorious diggers and can easily topple unstable rock piles by digging out the substrate beneath them.
- **Substrate**: Use a fine aragonite or sand. This helps buffer the pH and allows the cichlids to perform their natural sand-sifting behaviors.

## Water Parameters

African Cichlids require hard, alkaline water:
- **pH**: 7.8 – 8.6
- **Temperature**: 24°C – 28°C (75°F – 82°F)
- **Hardness**: 10 – 20 dGH

Regular water changes and robust filtration are crucial, as cichlids are heavy waste producers.
