A backyard koi pond is the ultimate landscape feature, bringing motion, color, and tranquility to any outdoor space. Unlike indoor aquariums, outdoor ponds interact directly with weather, sunlight, and local wildlife, requiring careful planning.

## Key Design Considerations

- **Depth**: Ponds should be at least 1 to 1.5 meters deep (3 to 5 feet) in the deepest areas. This prevents the pond from freezing completely in winter and provides a cool retreat during hot summer days.
- **Filtration**: A heavy-duty biofilter paired with a UV clarifier is essential. Koi grow large, eat a lot, and produce significant ammonia, which requires robust biological filtration to break down.
- **Predator Protection**: Incorporate vertical pond edges and caves/shelters for the fish to hide from herons, cats, and raccoons.

## Plants for Koi Ponds

Plants play a dual role in filtration and aesthetics:
- **Floating Plants**: Water lilies and water hyacinths block sunlight, reducing algae growth.
- **Marginal Plants**: Reeds, rushes, and irises along the edges filter nutrients.
