A paludarium blends the best of terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems, offering a home for both water-dwelling creatures and humidity-loving land plants. 

In this design guide, we explore how to construct a vertical setup that integrates a circular frame as a focal window, emphasizing the raw texture of rocky cliffs and flowing water.

## Structuring the Vertical Layout

When building vertically, weight and structural integrity are your primary concerns.

1. **The Eggcrate Grid**: Use light-diffusing eggcrate sheets to construct a vertical framework. This distributes the weight of the rocks and provides a grid to tie down driftwood and moss.
2. **Foaming and Shaping**: Apply aquarium-safe polyurethane expanding foam to build up the cliffs and secure the circular framing.
3. **Waterfall Plumbing**: Hide a small water pump and flexible tubing behind the eggcrate structure, directing the water flow to trickle down the center of the circular frame.

## Plant Selection

For the land section:
- **Java Moss & Christmas Moss**: Excellent for wrapping around wet rocks.
- **Anubias & Bucephalandra**: Thrive in the high humidity splash zones.
- **Miniature Ferns**: Placed at the dry upper regions.
