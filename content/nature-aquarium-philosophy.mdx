The Nature Aquarium style, pioneered by legendary photographer and aquascaper Takashi Amano, revolutionized the aquarium hobby. Rather than simply keeping fish, the Nature Aquarium focuses on recreating a miniature ecosystem that mirrors the harmony of terrestrial wild landscapes.

## Core Concepts of Nature Aquarium

- **Iwagumi Style**: An arrangement centered around rocks. It follows strict rules of odd-numbered stone placements (typically Oyaishi, Fukuishi, and Soishi) to create a spiritual, balanced landscape.
- **Ryuboku Style**: Built primarily with driftwood and mosses to mimic old forests and roots.
- **Wabi-Sabi**: Emphasizing impermanence, imperfection, and the organic aging process of the plants and wood over time.

## Fish Integration

In a Nature Aquarium, fish are selected to complement the landscape rather than dominate it. Large schools of small schooling fish (such as Rummy-nose Tetras, Cardinal Tetras, or Harlequin Rasboras) create a sense of scale and natural movement, leading to a deep sense of peaceful contemplation.
